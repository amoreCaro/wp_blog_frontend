10 dishes every visitor to Argentina needs to try

Argentina’s culinary scene is a feast for the senses, blending Italian, Spanish, and indigenous influences. From empanadas and choripán to asado and dulce de leche, these 10 iconic dishes are a must-try for any visitor exploring the flavors of Argentina.

January 11, 2026
  • 10 mins read

Argentina is a paradise for food lovers, offering a rich mix of flavors shaped by Italian and Spanish immigrants as well as local Andean and Patagonian ingredients. Here are 10 dishes every visitor should try: Empanadas – These stuffed dough pockets are Argentina’s favorite street food, baked or fried, with fillings like chicken, cheese, ham, sweetcorn, blue cheese, or beef. Regional specialties include quinoa and goat cheese in Jujuy or lamb in Patagonia. Choripán – A sausage sandwich served at asados (barbecues), often topped with chimichurri or salsa criolla. Sausages are usually pork but can include boar. Pizza – Argentinian pizza features inch-high crusts, minimal tomato sauce, and heaps of mozzarella cheese. Common toppings include green olives, oregano, or chili flakes. A cheese-and-tomato pizza is called una muzza, often eaten with a slice of faina (chickpea pancake). Milanesa – Thinly pounded beef or chicken, breaded and fried or baked. Variations include a caballo (with fried egg), a la napolitana (with cheese and tomato sauce), and a la suiza (with gruyère). Provoleta – Grilled provolone cheese, crispy on the outside and melted inside, often seasoned with oregano. Goat’s milk versions offer a creamier taste. Asado – Argentina’s famous barbecue and social event, featuring cuts like ribeye, sirloin, short ribs, and organ meats such as sweetbreads, chitterlings, and kidneys. Accompanied by choripán, blood sausage, and provoleta. Llama – Popular in Argentina’s northwest, llama meat is leaner than beef with a rustic, earthy flavor. Served as steak, in empanadas, stew, or even carpaccio. Humita – Corn-based snack or main dish, made with fresh corn, milk, onions, spices, and sometimes goat cheese. Steamed or boiled in corn leaves, it’s a pre-Columbian culinary tradition. Locro – A hearty stew eaten on May 25 for the May Revolution. Made with white corn, beef or pork, tripe, red chorizo, vegetables, and seasoned with cumin and bay leaf. Can be spiced with quiquirimichi, a paprika and chili salsa. Dulce de leche – Argentina’s iconic caramelized milk sauce, often eaten with flan or as a topping on thick, creamy ice cream. Dulce de leche helado is a must-try dessert treat. These dishes reflect Argentina’s rich culinary heritage and provide a delicious introduction to the country’s food culture, from street eats to traditional home cooking.
